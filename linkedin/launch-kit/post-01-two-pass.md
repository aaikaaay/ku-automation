## 📋 POST BODY (copy this verbatim)

> Most AI engineering reviews stop at the first answer.
>
> That's why most AI engineering reviews are unusable.
>
> When we built the AI reviewers behind KU Automation's portal — Principal Piping, Process, Mechanical, Civil, Structural — we discovered that what makes the output reach "consultant-grade" isn't a better model.
>
> It's a **second pass**.
>
> A critique pass. The same AI takes its own findings and brutalises them:
>
> ❌ "Is this finding actually code-cited, or am I just confident?"
> ❌ "Is the severity correct, or am I crying CRITICAL for an observation?"
> ❌ "Would a principal engineer write it this way?"
>
> The second pass typically kills 18-24% of weak findings, sharpens language on the rest, and forces explicit clause references (ASME B31.3, API 521, ADNOC PHL-SPC-001, etc.).
>
> The result: a deliverable a CEng can actually sign.
>
> I wrote up the full two-pass protocol — what the critique prompt looks like, where it fails, and the cost economics — on our blog.
>
> Link in the comments 👇
>
> Have you tried self-critique loops on your AI reviewers? What's killing weak findings for you?
