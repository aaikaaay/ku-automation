## 8. Custom Workflow Offer — How to Pitch It

The second offer in your master intro is **"if you have a workflow in mind that isn't on the list, I'll spec a free demo workflow for you"**. This is the deeper conversion lever.

### 8.1 How to qualify a custom workflow

When someone says "yes, I want a custom workflow", ask these 4 questions:

1. **What deliverable type / document does it start from?** (PDF? Excel? CAD? Multiple?)
2. **What does a 'pass' look like?** (Code compliance? Comparison to a spec? Cross-check between docs?)
3. **Who's the consumer of the output?** (Owner team for sign-off? Junior engineer learning? Client deliverable?)
4. **How many of these do you do per month?** (Determines pricing)

### 8.2 Common custom workflows to be ready to scope

| Ask | What it is | Effort to build |
|---|---|---|
| **Project-specific spec compliance** | AI reviews docs against client's project spec (not just universal codes) | 1-2 days (upload spec to KB) |
| **Cross-discipline interface check** | AI reads piping CAESAR II → flags inconsistencies in structural / civil | 3-5 days (multi-doc workflow) |
| **MTO reconciliation** | AI compares MTO Excel against P&ID + iso to find missing/excess items | 2-3 days |
| **Vendor doc review** | AI reviews vendor datasheets (e.g. pump quote) against owner's spec | 1 day |
| **Change order impact** | AI compares Rev N vs Rev N+1 of a deliverable and reports impact | 1-2 days |
| **Spec bot (knowledge bot)** | RAG chatbot trained on company's standards/specs/lessons learned | 1-2 weeks |
| **Procurement support** | AI reviews vendor TPI report against owner's ITP | 2-3 days |

### 8.3 Pricing custom workflows

Quote ranges in the DM (don't commit blindly):

- **Spec compliance review (KB-based)**: $2,000 - $5,000 one-time + $0.50-$2 per review
- **Custom multi-doc workflow**: $5,000 - $15,000 + per-review
- **Knowledge bot / RAG**: $8,000 - $25,000 one-time + $99-$499/mo hosting
- **Enterprise integration**: $25,000+

Frame it as: *"Step 1 — free demo workflow built on 2-3 sample docs. Step 2 — if it works, we productionise it."*

- **Demo → call <20%**: Demo report quality issue, or no clear ask in cover note.
- **Posts getting <50 impressions**: Posting at wrong time (use 8-9am or 7-9pm Dubai), or no hook in first line.

## 10. The DON'T List

- ❌ Don't pitch in the connection request. Just be polite + relevant.
- ❌ Don't auto-DM. LinkedIn detects it within ~50 sends and throttles your reach.
- ❌ Don't follow up more than 3 times. Move on.
- ❌ Don't post about yourself more than 1 of every 3 posts. Mix in industry value posts (no CTA).
- ❌ Don't argue in comments. Disagree politely, move on.
- ❌ Don't use #100hashtags. 3-5 max, all relevant.
- ❌ Don't post on Saturdays. Engineering audience is offline. Tue/Wed/Thu best.
- ❌ Don't send DMs at 3am their time. Use LinkedIn's timezone display, send between 8am-6pm theirs.
- ❌ Don't slag off competitors (even AI tools). Stay positive.
- ❌ Don't deliver a bad demo. Quality of the free demo IS the sales pitch.
