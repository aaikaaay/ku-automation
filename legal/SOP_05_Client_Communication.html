1. Purpose

This SOP establishes standards for professional, timely, and effective communication with clients throughout all engagements to ensure alignment, transparency, and client satisfaction.

2. Communication Principles

  • Professionalism: All communications reflect KU Automation's standards
  • Clarity: Use clear, jargon-free language; explain technical concepts simply
  • Timeliness: Respond promptly; never leave clients waiting
  • Transparency: Share progress honestly, including challenges
  • Proactivity: Anticipate questions and address them before asked

6. Email Standards

6.1 Email Structure

  • Subject: Clear, specific, include project name if applicable
  • Greeting: Professional salutation (Dear [Name], / Hi [Name],)
  • Body: Purpose first, then details, keep concise
  • Action Items: Clearly state any required actions
  • Closing: Professional sign-off with contact details

7. Meeting Standards

7.1 Before Meeting

  • Send calendar invite with clear agenda
  • Share relevant documents 24 hours in advance
  • Confirm attendees and their roles
  • Test technology (video, screen share) beforehand

7.2 During Meeting

  • Start on time
  • Begin with agenda overview
  • Take notes or assign note-taker
  • Stay on topic; park off-topic items
  • Summarize decisions and action items at end
  • End on time or agree to extend

7.3 After Meeting

  • Send meeting notes within 24 hours
  • Include: attendees, discussion summary, decisions, action items with owners/dates
  • Request confirmation of understanding if needed

8. Difficult Conversations

8.1 Delivering Bad News

  1. Communicate early - don't wait until the last minute
  2. Be direct and honest about the situation
  3. Explain the cause and impact
  4. Present a remediation plan
  5. Take responsibility where appropriate
  6. Follow up in writing after verbal discussion

8.2 Handling Complaints

  1. Listen fully without interrupting
  2. Acknowledge the client's concern
  3. Apologize if appropriate (without over-apologizing)
  4. Propose a resolution
  5. Follow up to ensure satisfaction
  6. Document the issue and resolution

10. Documentation

  • All significant client communications should be documented
  • Store in project folder or CRM
  • Verbal agreements must be confirmed in writing
  • Keep audit trail for key decisions
