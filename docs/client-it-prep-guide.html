🎯 Purpose of This Document

This is your personal preparation guide for engaging with client IT and security teams. It translates the client-facing IT & Security Requirements Guide into actionable talking points, anticipated questions, objection handlers, and meeting strategies.

Use this document to:

  • Prepare before client IT meetings
  • Anticipate tough questions and have answers ready
  • Handle security objections confidently
  • Guide conversations toward your preferred deployment options
  • Close the technical validation phase faster
💡 Key Insight

Client IT teams are gatekeepers, not decision-makers. Their job is to find reasons to say "no" or delay. Your job is to make "yes" the path of least resistance by being prepared, credible, and responsive.

The IT Approval Journey

Most client IT teams follow a predictable evaluation path. Knowing where you are helps you guide the conversation:

  1. Initial Review — IT receives your documentation, does a quick scan for red flags
  2. Technical Questions — Detailed Q&A on architecture, security, integration
  3. Security Assessment — Formal review against their policies (may include questionnaire)
  4. Proof of Concept — Sometimes requested before full approval
  5. Final Approval — Sign-off from IT leadership and/or security team

Your goal: Compress this timeline by being proactive with documentation and responsive to questions.

📋 Deployment Options — Your Positioning

You offer 4 deployment options. Here's how to position each based on the client's situation:

🌐 Option A: SaaS (Cloud)

Push when: Client wants speed, has no dedicated DevOps, or is doing a POC. Lowest friction.

Margin: Highest (no deployment overhead)

☁️ Option B: Private Cloud

Push when: Client has AWS/Azure/GCP presence and compliance requirements. Wants control + cloud benefits.

Margin: Good (some setup work)

🏢 Option C: On-Premises

Push when: Air-gapped environments, government/defence, or "no cloud ever" policy. Expect longer timeline.

Margin: Lower (significant deployment effort)

🔀 Option D: Hybrid

Push when: Client wants AI benefits but has strict data residency. Good compromise position.

Margin: Good (gateway simplifies)

💰 Commercial Tip

Always start by recommending Option A (SaaS) unless you already know they have restrictions. It's faster to deploy, easier to support, and higher margin. Let them push back to other options rather than volunteering complexity.

🔥 Anticipated Questions & Your Responses

These are the questions that come up in almost every IT conversation. Have these answers ready:

Security & Data Protection

Where is our data stored? Who has access to it?

Your data is stored in [EU Frankfurt / US Virginia — confirm based on contract]. Only your authorised users and our Tier 2+ support engineers (under strict access controls and audit logging) can access it. We operate on a least-privilege model — support access requires a ticket and is logged.

For Private Cloud or On-Prem deployments, data never leaves your infrastructure. You control all access.

How is data encrypted?

In transit: TLS 1.2 minimum, TLS 1.3 preferred. All API calls and web traffic are encrypted.

At rest: AES-256 encryption. For Private Cloud/On-Prem, you can use your own encryption keys (BYOK) — we support AWS KMS, Azure Key Vault, and HashiCorp Vault.

What compliance certifications do you have?

We're SOC 2 Type II certified (audit report available under NDA), ISO 27001 certified, and GDPR compliant. We can provide a Data Processing Agreement (DPA) for EU data subjects. For healthcare clients, we offer HIPAA-eligible deployment with a Business Associate Agreement (BAA).

Happy to share our SOC 2 report, pen test summary, or complete a security questionnaire (SIG, CAIQ, custom).

Have you had any security breaches?

No. We've had zero security breaches or data incidents. We run continuous vulnerability scanning, annual penetration tests by third-party firms, and have a responsible disclosure program. Our security posture is documented in our SOC 2 report.

Infrastructure & Integration

What ports and network access do you need?

For SaaS: Just outbound HTTPS (port 443) to *.ku-automation.com. No inbound ports required. Works through standard corporate proxies.

For Private/On-Prem: You control what's exposed. Typically HTTPS (443) for the web UI, and optionally SSH (22) for admin access from your internal network only.

Can you integrate with our SSO / identity provider?

Yes — we support SAML 2.0 and OpenID Connect (OIDC). We've integrated with Okta, Azure AD, Google Workspace, Ping Identity, and OneLogin. Configuration typically takes 1-2 hours. We can also enforce MFA through your IdP policies.

What's your uptime / SLA?

Our SaaS platform has a 99.9% uptime SLA (about 8.7 hours max downtime per year). We publish a status page at status.ku-automation.com. For Private Cloud/On-Prem, uptime depends on your infrastructure — we provide HA deployment guides and monitoring recommendations.

What happens to our data if we cancel?

You can export all your data at any time via our API or admin dashboard. Upon contract termination, we retain data for 30 days (in case you change your mind), then permanently delete it with certification available on request. For On-Prem, you control everything — just decommission the servers.

Support & Operations

What support do you provide?

Standard support: Email/ticket support, <4 hour response during business hours, access to documentation and knowledge base.

Premium support (available): 24/7 coverage, <1 hour response for critical issues, dedicated success manager, quarterly business reviews.

We also provide onboarding assistance and training sessions included in the implementation.

How do you handle updates and patches?

SaaS: We handle all updates automatically. Security patches are applied within 24-48 hours of release. Feature updates are rolled out gradually with advance notice.

Private/On-Prem: We provide update packages and release notes. You control when to apply them. Critical security patches come with an advisory and recommended timeline.

🛡️ Handling Objections

These are the common objections you'll face and how to overcome them:

⚠️ "We can't use cloud services — security policy prohibits it."

Why they say this: Often a blanket policy that hasn't been updated, or they've had a bad experience with a cloud vendor.

"I understand — many of our clients in [regulated industry] had similar policies. That's exactly why we offer on-premises deployment that runs entirely within your data centre with zero external connectivity. Your data never leaves your network.

Alternatively, our Private Cloud option deploys within your own AWS/Azure VPC — you control the infrastructure, encryption keys, and network policies. Many security teams approve this because it's effectively on-prem in the cloud."

⚠️ "We need to do a full security assessment — this will take months."

Why they say this: Covering themselves, or genuinely have a lengthy vendor review process.

"Completely understand — thorough security reviews are important. To help accelerate this, I can provide:

  • Our SOC 2 Type II report (covers most security questionnaire items)
  • A completed SIG Lite or CAIQ questionnaire
  • Our latest penetration test summary
  • Architecture diagrams and data flow documentation

If you share your questionnaire template, I'll prioritise getting it completed. Many clients have compressed their review to 2-3 weeks with this documentation."

⚠️ "What if you go out of business? We'd be stuck."

Why they say this: Vendor lock-in fear, especially with smaller companies.

"Valid concern. Here's how we mitigate that risk:

  • Data portability: Export your data anytime via API — standard formats, no proprietary lock-in
  • Source code escrow: Available for enterprise contracts (we can discuss)
  • Standard protocols: We use REST APIs, PostgreSQL, standard container formats — nothing proprietary
  • On-prem option: If you deploy on your infrastructure, you have the software regardless

We're committed to building a sustainable business, but your independence is protected either way."

⚠️ "We don't have the resources to deploy and maintain this."

Why they say this: IT team is stretched thin, don't want another system to babysit.

"That's exactly why our SaaS option is popular — zero deployment, zero maintenance on your side. We handle all infrastructure, updates, backups, and monitoring. Your team just uses it.

If you do need Private/On-Prem for policy reasons, we provide:

  • Managed deployment: Our team does the setup
  • Automated updates: One-click or scheduled update packages
  • Monitoring dashboards: We alert you before issues become problems
  • Optional managed services: We can run it for you even in your cloud

Typical IT overhead is less than 2 hours/month once deployed."

⚠️ "Your pricing is too high for what we're getting."

Why they say this: Sometimes genuine budget constraints, often a negotiation tactic.

"I understand budget is a factor. Let's look at the full picture:

  • Time savings: [X hours/week] of manual work automated — what's that worth?
  • Error reduction: AI catches issues humans miss — cost of one prevented mistake?
  • Scalability: Handle 10x the volume without hiring 10x the people

Most clients see ROI within [3-6 months]. I can run through the ROI calculator with specific numbers for your use case.

If budget is tight, we can explore a phased rollout or pilot program with lower upfront commitment."

🚨 Red Flags to Watch For

🚩 Warning Signs
  • "We'll need to build something custom" — They're trying to redesign your product. Redirect to standard deployment.
  • "Can you send us the source code to review?" — Unusual request. Offer SOC 2 and pen test instead. Escrow for enterprise only.
  • "We need to involve [5+ more stakeholders]" — Scope creep / delay tactic. Ask who has final sign-off authority.
  • No business sponsor in the room — IT can kill deals but rarely champions them. Make sure someone with budget authority is engaged.
  • "We've never approved a cloud vendor before" — Prepare for a long process. Consider offering a limited pilot to build trust.
